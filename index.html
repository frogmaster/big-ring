Big Ring Indoor Video Cycling

Cycle through amazing scenery, inside

Use Big Ring Indoor Video Cycling to make your indoor cycling training more interesting by being able to view amazing scenery from your bicycle in your pain cave.

Big Ring Indoor Video Cycling can be used in combination with a bicycle, indoor cycling trainer and ANT+ power, cadence or heart rate sensors.

Real Life Videos

Real Life Videos are videos of roads or paths which have metadata describing the elevation profile and distance of the course. Using this metadata, Big Ring can adjust the playing speed of the video to the speed at which the cyclist is riding.

Real Life Videos can be bought and/or downloaded from several places:

  • real-life-video.nl
  • reallifevideo.de
  • BikeLab
  • Cycling-RLV (free)
  • RLV-alucard (free)
  • and more
Both reallifevideo.de and BikeLab have high quality demos which you can use to try out this software before having to buy any videos.

How does it work?

To use Big Ring Indoor Cycling, you need a couple of attributes:

  • A Bicycle
  • An Indoor Bike Trainer
  • A PC (Windows or Linux)
  • One or more Real Life Videos
Using this setup, it's possible to configure a cycling power level (e.g. 250 Watts). Big Ring will determine the speed of the cyclist using the grade of the road, cyclist's weight, wind resistance and rolling resistance.

To make things more interesting, you either need an ANT+ power meter or an ANT+ speed sensor. To be able to use these with Big Ring, you will also need an ANT+ USB Stick (e.g. this one.) If you are using a power meter, Big Ring will again determine speed from grade of the road, cyclist's weight, wind resistance and rolling resistance, but now using the cyclist's real power as input.

When using a speed sensor, there are two possibilities. If you have trainer with a known power curve (currently only Cycleops Fluid 2 and Kurt Kinetic Fluid trainers are supported), you can use what is known as Virtual Power. Using Virtual Power, Big Ring determines the power output of the cyclist by calculating it using the speed of the back wheel.

If you have a speed sensor, but no supported trainer, Big Ring can use the speed of the back wheel directly. When cycling, you will have to adjust the resistance setting of the trainer yourself.
